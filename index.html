About this simulator

Borne from the death of Ella Adoo-Kissi-Debrah, who was the first to have air pollution listed on her death certificate in 2020, it is widely evident that air pollution is becoming a crippling burden on society and a leading cause of poor health internationally. More specifically, within London, this issue is exemplified by persistent exceedances of WHO air quality guidelines, with transportation accounting for approximately 50% of NOx emissions and significant contributions to particulate matter pollution.

Given this dire societal burden, a new technology has originated to absorb network congestion attributed to e-commerce: unmanned aerial vehicles. By lifting portions of our transport network into the sky, links will be more freely available for the likes of public transport and passenger movements.

What is UAM-ECS?
The Urban Air Mobility Environmental Cost Simulator (UAM-ECS) is an interactive decision tool designed to evaluate the environmental implications of introducing electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft for urban logistic routes. This simulator enables urban planners, policymakers, and mobility operators to assess the holistic environmental costs-including greenhouse gas emissions, energy consumption, noise pollution, and land-use impacts-associated with UAM deployment compared to conventional ground-based delivery systems.

How does it work?
UAM-ECS employs a parametric modelling approach that integrates real-world operational constraints and key parameters across various domains. Such examples encompass:

  • Route Scenario Parameters: Number of delivery nodes, nodal distances, parcel drop specifications, battery and payload swap times, and operating hours
  • Drone Parameters: Drone mass, ascending/descending power, rotor and wing specifications, battery characteristics, and component lifespans
  • Vertiport Parameters: Greenfield land fraction and ecology loss costs
  • Embodied Carbon: Material-specific embodied carbon intensities and masses for steel, nylon, aluminium, magnesium, copper, carbon fibre, fibreglass, and batteries
  • Ground Delivery Van Parameters: Van mass, battery capacity, driver costs, emissions factors, speed profiles, component lifespans, and route geometry
  • Other Parameters: Physical constants, power conversion efficiencies, carbon costs, aerodynamic coefficients, and damage cost factors

The simulator calculates trip-level environmental impacts and extrapolates these across operational timescales (2026-2050) to visualize trajectories under different deployment scenarios. Environmental costs are expressed as monetized values (USD) using their respective social costs. The simulator stretches to 2050, in line with the Net Zero goals of the Paris Climate Agreement.

Research Foundation
This tool is grounded in thesis research conducted at New York University's Tandon School of Engineering in collaboration with MIT's JTL-Urban Mobility Lab. The underlying methodology synthesiaes lifecycle assessment (LCA) frameworks and environmental economics to identify inflection points for which drone deployment becomes environmentally beneficial.

Intended Use
UAM-ECS is designed for exploratory scenario analysis and educational purposes. It helps stakeholders understand trade-offs, identify critical parameters that drive environmental outcomes, and develop strategies for environmentally responsible UAM deployment. The tool encourages evidence-based discourse around emerging mobility technologies and their role in sustainable urban futures.

About the Developer
Jasmine Brittan is an MS student in Transportation Systems, with a concentration in Mobility Systems Engineering. Her career thus far has embodied various internships within aerial mobility and transportation, including at the European Space Agency, NASA and ZF Automotive.
